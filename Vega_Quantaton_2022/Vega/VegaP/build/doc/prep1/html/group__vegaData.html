Computes the Nelson-Siegel discount curve:

\[ d(t) = e^{-\gamma(t)(t-t_0)}, \quad t\geq t_0, \]

where the yield curve has the form:

\[ \gamma(t) = c_0 + c_1 \frac{1-e^{-\lambda(t-t_0)}}{\lambda (t-t_0)} + c_2 \left(\frac{1-e^{-\lambda(t-t_0)}}{\lambda (t-t_0)} - e^{-\lambda (t-t_0)}\right), \quad t\geq t_0. \]

Parameters
dC0\(c_0\) The first constant
dC1\(c_1\) The second constant.
dC2\(c_2\) The third constant.
dLambda\(\lambda>0\) The mean-reversion rate.
dInitialTime\(t_0\) The initial time.
Returns
The yield curve for the Nelson-Siegel model.

Computes a discount curve by linear interpolation of market yields. The construction of the discount curve is accomplished in 3 steps:

  1. We obtain the market yields from discount factors rDF.
  2. We get the market yield curve by linear interpolation.
  3. We compute the discount curve from the yield curve.
Parameters
rTimesThe maturities of market discount factors.
rDFThe market discount factors.
dRThe initial short-term interest rate.
dInitialTimeThe initial time.
Returns
The discount curve obtained from the market discount factors by the linear interpolation of market yields.

Computes the curve of forward prices ("clean" or "dirty") for a coupon bond. The bond pays coupons \(q\delta t\) at times \((t_i)_{i=1,\dots,M}\) such that

\[ t_0 < t_1 \leq t_0+\delta t, \quad t_{i+1}-t_i= \delta t, \quad t_M = T. \]

The bond also pays notional \(N=1\) at maturity \(T\). The buyer of the forward contract pays forward price \(F(t)\) at delivery time \(t\) and then receives coupons \(q\delta t\) at payments times \(t_i>t\) and notional \(N=1\) at maturity \(T\).

Parameters
dRate\(q\) The coupon rate.
dPeriod\(\delta t\) The time interval between payments.
dMaturity\(T\) The maturity.
rDiscountThe discount curve.
bCleanThis parameter controls the output. If its value is true, then we compute "clean" prices. Otherwise, we compute "dirty" prices that include the accrued interest from the next coupon.
Returns
The forward price ("clean" or "dirty") curve for the coupon bond.
