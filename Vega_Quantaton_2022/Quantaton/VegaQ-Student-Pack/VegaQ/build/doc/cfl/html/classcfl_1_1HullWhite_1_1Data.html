Detailed Description

The parameters of Hull and White model.

This class defines the parameters of the Hull-White model for interest rates. The set of parameters consists of discount, shape, and volatility curves. We denote

  • \(B(t,T)\) the discount factor computed at \(t\) for maturity \(T\),
  • \(F(t,T,U) = B(t,U)/B(t,T)\) the forward price at \(t\) in the contract expiring at \(T\) and written on the discount factor with maturity \(U>T\).

The forward prices evolve as

\[ dF(t,T,U) = F(t,T,U)(A(U)-A(T))\sigma(t) dW^T_t, \quad t\in [t_0,T], \quad F(t_0,T,U) = B(t_0,U)/B(t_0,T) \text{ is given}, \]

where

  • \(A=A(T)\), \(T\geq t_0\), is the shape function, \(A(t_0) = 0\), \(A'(t_0) = 1\).
  • \(\sigma(t)\) is the normalized volatility at \(t\). The true volatility for forward price \(F(t,T,U)\) is \((A(U)-A(T))\sigma(t)\).
  • \(t_0\) is the initial time.
  • \(W^T = (W^T_t)\) is a Brownian motion under the forward martingale measure corresponding to maturity \(T\).

◆ volatility

Function cfl::HullWhite::Data::volatility

The average normalized volatility curve

\[ \Sigma(T) = \frac1{T-t_0} \sqrt{\int_{t_0}^T \sigma^2(t) dt}, \quad T\geq t_0. \]

The implied volatility of options expiring at \(T\) and written on the discount factor with maturity \(U>T\) is \((A(U)-A(T))\Sigma(T)\).
