Constructs the parameters of the Hull-White model for interest rates. We denote

  • \(B(t,T)\) the discount factor computed at \(t\) for maturity \(T\),
  • \(F(t,T,U) = B(t,U)/B(t,T)\) the forward price at \(t\) in the contract expiring at \(T\) and written on the discount factor with maturity \(U>T\).

The forward prices evolve as

\[ dF(t,T,U) = F(t,T,U)(A(U)-A(T))\sigma(t) dW^T_t, \quad t\in [t_0,T], \quad F(t_0,T,U) = B(t_0,U)/B(t_0,T) \text{ is given}, \]

where

  • \(A=A(T)\), \(T\geq t_0\), is the shape function, \(A(t_0) = 0\), \(A'(t_0) = 1\).
  • \(\sigma(t)\) is the normalized volatility at \(t\). The true volatility for forward price \(F(t,T,U)\) is \((A(U)-A(T))\sigma(t)\).
  • \(t_0\) is the initial time.
  • \(W^T = (W^T_t)\) is a Brownian motion under the forward martingale measure corresponding to maturity \(T\).
Parameters
rDiscountInitial discount curve \(B(t_0,T)\), \(T\geq t_0\).
rVolatilityAverage normalized volatility curve

\[ \Sigma(T) = \frac1{T-t_0} \sqrt{\int_{t_0}^T \sigma^2(t) dt}, \quad T\geq t_0. \]

The implied volatility of options expiring at \(T\) and written on the discount factor with maturity \(U>T\) is \((A(U)-A(T))\Sigma(T)\).
rShapeInitial shape curve \(A(T)\), \(T\geq t_0\), \(A(t_0) = 0\), \(A'(t_0) = 1\). It describes the form of changes in the yield and discount curves.
dInitialTimeThe initial time given as year fraction.

Constructs the parameters of the Hull-White model for interest rates with stationary volatility curve determined by positive constants \(\kappa\) (the volatility of the short rate) and \(\lambda\) (the mean-reversion of the short rate). We denote

  • \(B(t,T)\) the discount factor computed at \(t\) for maturity \(T\),
  • \(F(t,T,U) = B(t,U)/B(t,T)\) the forward price at \(t\) in the contract expiring at \(T\) and written on the discount factor with maturity \(U>T\).

The forward prices evolve as

\[ dF(t,T,U) = F(t,T,U)(e^{-\lambda (U-t)} - e^{-\lambda (T-t)}) \frac{\kappa}{\lambda} dW^T_t, \quad t\in [t_0,T], \quad F(t_0,T,U) = B(t_0,U)/B(t_0,T) \text{ is given}, \]

The shape function has the form:

\[ A(t) = \frac{1-e^{-\lambda (t-t_0)}}{\lambda}, \quad t\geq t_0. \]

The normalized volatility function is given by

\[ \sigma(t) = \kappa e^{ \lambda (t-t_0)}, \quad t\geq t_0. \]

The average normalized volatility curve has the form:

\[ \Sigma(T) = \kappa\sqrt{\frac{\exp(2\lambda(T-t_0)-1}{2\lambda(T-t_0)}}, \quad T\geq t_0. \]

The implied volatility at \(t\) for options expiring at \(T\) written on the discount factor with maturity \(U>T\) has the form:

\[ \Psi(t,T,U) = \kappa \frac{1-\exp(-\lambda(U-T))}{\lambda} \sqrt{\frac{1 - \exp(-2\lambda(T-t))}{2\lambda(T-t)}}, \quad t_0\leq t< T. \]

dLambdaThe mean-reversion coefficient of the spot price.
